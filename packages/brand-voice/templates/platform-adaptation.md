# Platform Voice Adaptation

Same brand voice. Different expression per platform. This isn't inconsistency — it's fluency.

## Core Voice (The Constant)

These never change regardless of platform:
- Your actual opinions
- Your vocabulary level and precision
- Your lack of corporate-speak
- Your specificity with claims

## X (Twitter)

**Constraint:** 280 characters. Every word earns its place.

**Adaptations:**
- Strip all hedging — you have no room for "kind of" and "sort of"
- One idea per tweet. One.
- Strong first sentence = the only thing most people read
- Use threads for multi-point ideas; each tweet must stand alone
- Rhetorical questions work here more than elsewhere

**What doesn't work:**
- Nuance without setup (needs too much context)
- Anything that requires you to finish the sentence in the reply

**Format check:**
- No more than 2 hashtags (usually 0)
- Abbreviation only if it genuinely fits (not because you ran out of room)
- Links eat characters — factor in t.co shortening

---

## Substack / Long-Form Newsletter

**Constraint:** Readers opted in; you have their time, but they'll leave at the first sign of filler.

**Adaptations:**
- Can show working — reasoning, dead ends, uncertainty
- More personal asides are appropriate
- Build more slowly before landing the main point
- Subheadings become essential navigation
- Pull quotes help scanners find the substance

**What doesn't work:**
- Still no filler — just more room for substance
- Don't mistake length for depth

**Format check:**
- 1000-3000 words sweet spot
- Paragraphs ≤ 4 sentences
- At least one subheading per major section
- Strong subject line (tests like a tweet)

---

## LinkedIn

**Constraint:** Professional context. Readers are career-aware.

**Adaptations:**
- Lead with the practical implication (what does this mean for someone's career/business?)
- More personal narrative is appropriate and expected
- Opinion is fine, but earned with experience evidence
- End with a genuine question to the audience

**What doesn't work:**
- Engagement bait ("Drop a ❤️ if...")
- Hashtag spam (2-3 max, only if genuinely searchable)
- Humble-bragging disguised as insight

**Format check:**
- 400-1200 words
- Short paragraphs (LinkedIn compresses them — preview matters)
- No walls of text

---

## Bluesky

**Constraint:** 300 characters. More community-oriented than X.

**Adaptations:**
- Slightly more conversational than X
- Self-replies for threads work better here (tighter community reads threads)
- Technical audiences are more tolerant of specificity

**What doesn't work:**
- Pure promotion without adding value
- Self-promotion without context

---

## Facebook (Personal or Page)

**Constraint:** Conversational network; different reader relationship.

**Adaptations:**
- More first-person, personal voice ("I've been thinking about...")
- Story setups work well ("Last week, something weird happened with...")
- Shorter paragraphs — Facebook's Lexical editor compresses line breaks
- Link in first comment if you don't want the algorithm to suppress it

**What doesn't work:**
- Formal language — feels weird here
- Technical deep dives without personal entry point

---

## Adaptation Principle

The question isn't "how do I sound different on each platform?"

The question is: "How would I tell this same story to a different room of people?"

Your voice doesn't change. The framing does.
