# De-AI-ify Wordlist

Words and phrases that signal AI-generated content. If any appear in a final draft: rewrite the sentence, don't just delete the word.

Deleting leaves a gap. Rewriting finds what you actually meant to say.

## Tier 1 — Hard Removes (Always)

These words almost never appear in genuine human writing at the frequency AI uses them:

- **delve** — use "explore", "examine", or just say what you're doing
- **utilize** — use "use"
- **leverage** (as a verb without the physical meaning) — use "use", "apply", "tap into"
- **robust** — use specific words for what makes it strong
- **comprehensive** — use specific words for what it covers
- **holistic** — usually meaningless; say what you mean
- **nuanced** — show the nuance, don't name it
- **paradigm** — almost always pretentious
- **synergy** — never
- **unpack** — use "explain", "break down", or just do the thing
- **dive into** — use "look at", or just start
- **in conclusion** — cut it; your conclusion should be evident
- **furthermore** — cut it or use "also", "and", "next"
- **it is worth noting** — just note it
- **it is important to note** — just note it
- **as we've explored** — cut it
- **in today's [world/landscape/era]** — cut it; start with the actual point

## Tier 2 — AI Vocal Tells (Rewrite)

Phrases that betray chatbot behavior:

- **Certainly!** — delete; start with the answer
- **Absolutely!** — delete
- **Great question!** — delete
- **I'd be happy to** — delete; just do it
- **I'd love to help** — delete
- **Of course!** — delete
- **Sure thing!** — delete
- **That's a great point** — delete; respond to the point
- **I understand** (as filler) — cut or replace with substance
- **Exciting** (applied to things that aren't exciting) — cut or replace with specifics
- **Fascinating** — cut
- **Innovative** — cut; say what makes it new
- **Game-changing** — cut; say what changes

## Tier 3 — Overused Transitions (Replace)

These aren't necessarily AI tells, but they're lazy:

- **However** → cut or use "but"
- **Therefore** → "so"
- **Additionally** → "also" or start a new paragraph
- **Moreover** → "also"
- **Subsequently** → "then" or "next" or "after"
- **In order to** → "to"
- **Due to the fact that** → "because"
- **At this point in time** → "now"
- **In the event that** → "if"

## Tier 4 — AI Essay Structure

These structural patterns reveal AI thinking:

- **The intro that summarizes what you're about to say** — cut; start with the content
- **"We'll explore..."** — just explore
- **Three-part listicles with identical parallel structure** — OK to use, but vary them
- **Every paragraph the same length** — vary sentence and paragraph length
- **Ending with "In summary, [repeats what you just said]"** — replace with a landing punch

## The Test

Read your piece out loud. If it sounds like a well-behaved chatbot, it needs more work.

If you'd be comfortable saying every sentence in a conversation with a smart friend: it's clean.
