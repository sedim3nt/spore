# Rate Calculator and Project Scoping Guide

Figure out what to charge and how to scope it cleanly.

## Hourly Rate Calculation

Start with your target annual income:

```
Target take-home: $[X]
Self-employment tax (~15.3%): add 20% to gross
Health insurance, benefits: add $[X]/year
Business expenses: add $[X]/year
Overhead total: $[total]

Billable hours/year:
  52 weeks × 40 hrs = 2,080 hrs
  Minus: vacation (2 wks × 40) = 80
  Minus: admin/sales/non-billable (~30%) = 600
  Realistic billable: ~1,400 hours/year

Minimum hourly rate: Overhead ÷ 1,400 = $[X]/hr
Target rate (market × quality premium): $[X]/hr
```

## Project Flat-Rate Formula

For fixed-price projects:

```
Estimated hours × 1.3 (buffer) × hourly rate = base price
Add: 10-20% complexity premium for ambiguous scope
Add: 10% for difficult client signals
Result: your flat-rate quote
```

**Never** quote flat-rate without a defined scope. Ambiguous scope = scope creep = underearning.

## Project Scoping Checklist

Before quoting any project, get answers to:

**What:**
- [ ] What are the specific deliverables? (not "a website" — what pages, what features?)
- [ ] What format? (file type, dimensions, platform)
- [ ] Who decides when it's done?

**When:**
- [ ] When do they need it by? (real deadline vs. "ASAP")
- [ ] When can they provide feedback? (turnaround time affects your timeline)
- [ ] Are there dependency deadlines? (conference, launch date, etc.)

**Who:**
- [ ] Who is the single point of contact?
- [ ] Who has final approval authority?
- [ ] How many stakeholders give feedback? (more = more revisions)

**Risk factors (add buffer for each YES):**
- [ ] Client has changed direction on similar projects before?
- [ ] Scope is described vaguely or still evolving?
- [ ] Multiple decision-makers with different opinions?
- [ ] Rush timeline?
- [ ] Novel technology or domain you haven't worked in before?

## When to Raise Your Rates

Raise your rates when:
- You're booked > 80% of available time for 2+ months
- You're turning down work because you're full
- Clients accept quotes without negotiating
- You've added new skills or credentials
- Market rates have risen in your category

**Rule:** Raise rates on new clients first. Raise rates on returning clients with 60 days notice.

## Discounting Policy

Don't discount your rate — discount the scope instead.

If client can't afford full price:
1. Reduce scope to fit their budget
2. Offer a payment plan (same total)
3. Defer non-essential phases

Never reduce your rate — it anchors their expectations permanently.
