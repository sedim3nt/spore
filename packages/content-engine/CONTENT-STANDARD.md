# Content Quality Standard

Every piece of content must pass all 10 points before publishing. This is a checklist, not a suggestion.

---

## The 10-Point Check

### 1. Does it have a clear point?
Not a topic — a point. "The future of AI" is a topic. "AI agents will make solo operators 10x more productive in 18 months, and here's the specific evidence" is a point.

**Fail signal:** You can't state the article's thesis in one sentence.

---

### 2. Does it deliver on the headline?
The headline makes a promise. The body cashes it. If the headline says "5 ways to X," there are 5 specific, usable ways.

**Fail signal:** The headline oversells; the content is vague or hedged.

---

### 3. Is the opening strong?
The first 2 sentences must earn the reader's next 5 minutes. No slow wind-ups, no background context they didn't ask for.

**Fail signal:** The article starts with "In today's fast-paced world..." or equivalent.

---

### 4. Is it specific?
Concrete over abstract. Numbers over generalities. Examples over principles.

- ❌ "Results improved significantly"
- ✅ "Revenue grew 40% in 90 days"

**Fail signal:** You could replace the examples with any other examples and the point would be identical.

---

### 5. Is the voice consistent?
Reads like one person wrote it, not three. No tonal whiplash between sections.

**Fail signal:** Paragraph 2 is casual, paragraph 4 is formal, paragraph 6 is corporate.

---

### 6. Is it de-AI-ified?
No AI tells. If readers can tell this was written by a machine, it's not done.

Strip: "certainly", "absolutely", "I'd be happy to", "it's important to note", "in conclusion", "furthermore", "delve", "utilize" (use "use"), "leverage" (unless literal), "robust"

**Fail signal:** You find any of the above words in the final draft.

---

### 7. Does it pass the "so what?" test?
After each section, ask: so what? Why does this matter? Who cares?

**Fail signal:** You can remove a section and the article is just as good.

---

### 8. Is the ending purposeful?
The ending does one of: (a) calls to action, (b) lands the thesis with force, (c) opens a genuine question. Not a summary of what you just said.

**Fail signal:** The last paragraph starts with "In summary..." or "As we've seen..."

---

### 9. Is the formatting clean?
- Headers are logical and scannable
- Paragraphs are 2-4 sentences max
- Lists are used for actual lists (not to avoid writing)
- No walls of text

**Fail signal:** Any paragraph longer than 6 lines.

---

### 10. Is it platform-appropriate?
Correct length, format, and tone for the target platform.

| Platform | Length | Format |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Substack | 1000-3000 words | Headers, paragraphs, pull quotes |
| X/Twitter | 280 chars per tweet | Threads max 10 |
| Bluesky | 300 chars | Short, standalone or thread |
| LinkedIn | 300-1500 words | Short paragraphs, no walls |

**Fail signal:** Substack article reads like a tweet thread; tweet reads like an essay.

---

## Final Gate

If any point fails: rewrite until it passes. 9 out of 10 is not a pass.
